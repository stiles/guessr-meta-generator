Identity & symbols

Language

Tok Pisin, Hiri Motu, and English are commonly spoken. The alphabet is similar to the English alphabet with some local variations.

Culture

Distinctive cultural markers include traditional markets, tribal art, and signs of local practices and festivals. Look for traditional dress and crafts.

Miscellaneous

Look for palm trees, volcanic landscapes, and the presence of local wildlife. The presence of informal roadside stalls selling local goods can also be indicative.

Capital

Port Moresby

Environment & landscape

Environment

The landscape includes tropical rainforests, mountains, and coastal areas. There are many islands and a mix of urban and rural settings.

Infrastructure

Buildings often reflect traditional Melanesian architecture with thatched roofs, bamboo structures, and modern concrete buildings in urban areas.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates typically have a white background with black lettering. Plates may include the letters 'PNG' to indicate Papua New Guinea.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are often in English or Tok Pisin, with a mix of metric and imperial units. Signs may feature bright colors and pictograms.
