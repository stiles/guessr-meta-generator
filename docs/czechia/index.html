Czechia

Home > Europe > Eastern Europe > Czechia

Language

Czech is the official language, using the Latin alphabet with diacritics (e.g., á, č, ď, ě, ň, ó, ŕ, š, ť, ú, ž).

Cars

Vehicle registration plates have a white background with a blue band on the left containing the EU flag and the country code 'CZ'.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are generally white with blue or green backgrounds and include both Czech and EU-standard symbols.

Environment

Czechia features a mix of urban areas, rolling hills, forests, and rivers, with notable regions like the Bohemian Paradise and Moravian Karst.

Infrastructure

Architectural styles include Gothic, Baroque, and modern designs, with key features such as castles, cathedrals, and well-preserved historic towns.

Culture

Distinctive markers include traditional Czech architecture, local cuisine (like trdelník), and cultural events like folk festivals.

Bonus

Look for the prevalence of red-tiled roofs, cobblestone streets, and the presence of public transport trams in cities like Prague.
