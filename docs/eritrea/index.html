Eritrea

Language

Tigrinya, Arabic; Latin script is used along with Ge'ez script for Tigrinya.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates often have a blue background with white letters and numbers.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in Tigrinya and Arabic, with simple pictograms and often have a rustic look.

Environment

Characterized by a mix of coastal plains, mountainous regions, and the Danakil Depression; generally dry and arid.

Infrastructure

Architecture includes a mix of traditional Eritrean styles and Italian colonial influences; buildings often have earthy tones.

Culture

Presence of traditional markets, unique coffee ceremonies, and elements of Eritrean music and dance.

Bonus

Look for distinctively Eritrean elements such as the unique local architecture and the presence of traditional livestock.
