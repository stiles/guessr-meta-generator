Identity & symbols

Language

Romanian is the official language, written in the Latin alphabet. Russian is also widely spoken, particularly in the breakaway region of Transnistria.

Culture

Look for traditional Moldovan motifs in decorations, local markets selling wine and food, and the presence of Orthodox churches.

Miscellaneous

The presence of vineyards and wine cellars, especially in areas like Cricova or Milestii Mici, can be distinctive. Street names and shop signs may also reflect Romanian heritage.

Environment & landscape

Environment

The landscape includes rolling hills, vineyards, and agricultural fields. There are also stretches of forest and river valleys.

Infrastructure

Urban areas feature Soviet-era architecture alongside modern buildings. Common elements include apartment blocks and traditional houses with tiled roofs.

Topography

Moldova is mainly flat to rolling plains, with some hilly areas in the northern regions and a few small hills in the central part.

Road signs are generally in Romanian, with standard European-style signage. Some signs may include Cyrillic characters in Transnistria.
