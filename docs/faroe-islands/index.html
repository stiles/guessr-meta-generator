Faroe Islands

Language

Faroese is the official language, using the Latin alphabet with special characters such as ð (eth) and ø (o-slash).

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band on the left featuring the country code 'FO'.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are typically in Faroese, with many using the Latin alphabet. Signs are often triangular or rectangular with a blue, white, or yellow background.

Environment

The landscape features rugged mountains, cliffs, and coastal areas, with a mix of green valleys and small villages.

Infrastructure

Buildings are often made of wood with turf roofs or painted in bright colors, reflecting Scandinavian architectural styles.

Culture

Look for traditional grass-roofed houses, distinctive sheep farming, and local Faroese flags displayed.

Bonus

Street View may show spectacular fjords, traditional wooden boats, and panoramic views over the archipelago.
