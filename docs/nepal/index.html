Nepali is the official language, written in the Devanagari script. English is also commonly used.

Culture

Cultural markers include unique festivals, traditional dress like the daura-suruwal, and the presence of stupas and temples.

Miscellaneous

Look for distinctive street vendors, colorful prayer flags, and the presence of the Himalayas in the background.

Environment

Foliage

Common tree types include Sal, Pine, and various fruit trees like mango and banana. Grasses vary by region, with tall grasses found in the Terai.

Topography

Nepal is predominantly mountainous, featuring the Himalayas, including Mount Everest, interspersed with hilly regions and the flat Terai plains.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a red border and have a combination of letters and numbers in Nepali script.

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Road signs are primarily in Nepali, with some bilingual signs in English. They often use Devanagari script.

Stop signs in Nepal typically read 'STOP' in English.

Bollards in Nepal are generally simple and may not have any distinctive designs; they are primarily used to control vehicle access.

Telephone poles in Nepal are often made of wood or concrete, and may have various wires for electricity, telephone, and internet.
