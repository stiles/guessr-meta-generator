Cape Verde

Language

Portuguese is the official language, with Cape Verdean Creole (Kriolu) being widely spoken. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates typically have a white background with blue lettering, featuring the letters 'CV' indicating Cape Verde.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are similar to those in Portugal, using Portuguese language and standard European road sign designs.

Environment

The landscape consists of volcanic islands with rugged mountains, sandy beaches, and arid plains, often with a dry climate.

Infrastructure

Architectural styles include a mix of colonial Portuguese buildings, modern structures, and traditional thatched houses. Urban areas may show a blend of concrete and colorful facades.

Culture

Distinctive markers include traditional music styles like morna and coladeira, local markets, and festivals celebrating Cape Verdean heritage.

Bonus

Look for unique local vegetation such as palm trees and cacti, as well as the absence of lush greenery typical of tropical regions.
