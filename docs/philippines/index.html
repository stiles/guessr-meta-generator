Philippines

Language

Filipino (Tagalog) is the official language, with English also widely used. The alphabet is Latin-based, with some special characters like 'Ã±' and 'ng'.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates have a white background with a blue band on top that contains the word 'Philippines' and the region code. They also feature a series of alphanumeric characters.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are typically in English and Filipino, with a mix of standard international symbols. Speed limits and directional signs are commonly seen.

Environment

The landscape varies from tropical beaches to mountainous regions, with lush greenery and urban areas featuring a mix of high-rise buildings and residential homes.

Infrastructure

Architectural styles range from colonial Spanish influences to modern structures. Urban areas have a mix of traditional and contemporary designs with vibrant colors.

Culture

Distinctive markers include jeepneys (colorful public transport vehicles), tricycles, and vibrant street markets. Festivals and street food stalls are commonly found.

Bonus

Look for the presence of tropical vegetation, such as coconut trees, and the occasional presence of sari-sari stores (small neighborhood shops).
