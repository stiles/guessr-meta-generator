Zimbabwe

Language

English is the official language, with Shona and Sindebele as widely spoken indigenous languages. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue strip on the left. The first letter represents the province, followed by a number and letters.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are mostly in English, with standard international symbols. Signs often include a mix of local languages for context.

Environment

The landscape features savannas, plateaus, and mountains, with notable wildlife and vegetation typical of southern Africa.

Infrastructure

Urban areas have a mix of colonial and modern architecture, with brick and concrete buildings. Rural areas may feature traditional thatched huts.

Culture

Look for traditional markets, art, and crafts, as well as wildlife motifs. The presence of the Zimbabwean flag or national symbols can also be a clue.

Bonus

Street view may show unique landmarks like Victoria Falls, the Great Zimbabwe ruins, and distinct wildlife parks.
