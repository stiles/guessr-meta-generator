Zambia

Language

English is the official language, with local languages such as Bemba, Nyanja, and Tonga also spoken. The alphabet is Latin-based with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band at the top, displaying the letters 'Z' followed by a combination of numbers and letters.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in English, with white and green colors for informational signs and red for warning signs. The signs often include pictograms.

Environment

Zambia features a mix of savannah, plateaus, and riverine landscapes, with the Zambezi River being a significant geographical feature.

Infrastructure

Urban areas have a mix of colonial-era buildings and modern structures, with brick and concrete materials being common. Rural areas are characterized by simpler, thatched-roof houses.

Culture

Look for traditional markets, local crafts, and the presence of wildlife-themed art. Cultural festivals may also be depicted in street views.

Bonus

Wildlife signs indicating nearby national parks, and presence of traditional huts or rural landscapes can also be indicators.
