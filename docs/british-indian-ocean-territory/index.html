British Indian Ocean Territory

Language

English is the official language; uses the Latin alphabet with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band on the left, featuring the Union Jack and the text 'British Indian Ocean Territory'.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are in English and generally follow UK standards, with clear, simple designs and reflective materials.

Environment

Tropical islands with white sandy beaches, turquoise waters, palm trees, and coral reefs. The landscape is flat with some elevated areas.

Infrastructure

Limited infrastructure, primarily military and research facilities; buildings are often functional with a mix of modern and colonial styles.

Culture

The territory is primarily a military base with little local culture; signs of British influence are evident, including pub names and British-style architecture.

Bonus

Sparse vegetation and clear ocean views; very few urban areas; mostly undeveloped land with some military installations.
