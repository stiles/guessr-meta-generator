Tuvalu

Language

English is the official language, with Tuvaluan and Samoan also spoken. The alphabet is Latin-based without any special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border, and they typically feature the letters 'TV' for Tuvalu.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are in English and often use simple pictograms. Warning signs may include local language translations.

Environment

Tuvalu is characterized by low-lying islands, coral atolls, sandy beaches, and lush vegetation. The landscape is generally flat with limited elevation.

Infrastructure

Buildings are typically simple, often made from local materials. Structures may include traditional thatched huts as well as modern concrete buildings.

Culture

Cultural markers include traditional attire, local crafts, and community gatherings. You may also see flags or symbols representing the islands' heritage.

Bonus

Google Street View may show local events, traditional ceremonies, and wildlife unique to the Pacific Islands, such as sea turtles or various bird species.
