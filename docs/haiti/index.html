Haiti

Language

French is the official language, with Haitian Creole widely spoken; the alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue stripe at the top; they often have a combination of letters and numbers.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in French, with some in Haitian Creole; signs may be less standardized and can vary in quality.

Environment

Haiti features mountainous terrain, coastal areas, and tropical vegetation; you may see a mix of urban and rural landscapes.

Infrastructure

Buildings often have a mix of colonial, modern, and local architectural styles; urban areas may have colorful houses and informal settlements.

Culture

Look for vibrant street life, colorful markets, and occasional public art; music and cultural festivals may also be evident.

Bonus

Check for the presence of tap-taps (colorful public transportation vehicles) and the general vibrancy of street scenes.
