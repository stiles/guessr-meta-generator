Identity & symbols

Language

Arabic is the official language, using the Arabic script. English is also widely used.

Culture

Distinctive markers include traditional markets (souks), Sudanese coffee houses, and local customs such as traditional clothing like the tobe.

Miscellaneous

Look for palm trees, traditional mud-brick architecture, and the presence of the Nile River in urban areas.

Capital

Khartoum

Environment & landscape

Environment

The landscape is characterized by deserts, particularly the Nubian Desert, and the Nile River running through the country.

Infrastructure

Architectural styles include a mix of traditional Sudanese mud brick buildings, modern concrete structures, and Islamic architecture.

Coverage

False

Foliage

Common tree types include acacia and tamarind, with sparse grasses in arid regions.

Topography

The major topographical characteristics include flat plains, plateaus, and some hilly areas, particularly in the southeastern regions.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border; the first number indicates the state or region.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in Arabic, with some signs also in English, often featuring Arabic script alongside Latin alphabet.

Stop

Stop signs typically read 'قف' (pronounced 'qif') in Arabic.

Bollards

Roadside bollards are generally simple, often painted in bright colors to enhance visibility.

Poles

Utility poles are mostly wooden, with some concrete poles in urban areas, often supporting both electrical and telephone wires.
