Syria

Language

Arabic is the official language, using the Arabic script. French and English are also commonly spoken, especially in urban areas.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue band at the top. The letters and numbers are often in Arabic script.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in Arabic, with some bilingual signs in English and French in urban areas. Signs can be irregular and may show signs of wear.

Environment

Syria features diverse landscapes, including mountains, deserts, and coastal areas. The terrain can be arid, with olive groves and agricultural fields in some regions.

Infrastructure

Architecture ranges from ancient ruins to modern buildings. Traditional homes may have arched doorways, while cities have a mix of modern and Ottoman-style structures.

Culture

Cultural markers include traditional markets (souks), mosques with distinctive minarets, and local street food stalls.

Bonus

Look for signs of conflict, such as damaged buildings or military presence. Also, the presence of ancient historical sites like Palmyra can be a clue.
