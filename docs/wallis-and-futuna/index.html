Wallis and Futuna

Language

French is the official language, with Wallisian and Futunan also spoken. The alphabet is Latin with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border and feature the letters 'WF' for Wallis and Futuna.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in French, with some local language signs. Signs are generally simple and may include symbols or images.

Environment

The islands have a tropical climate with lush vegetation, coral reefs, and volcanic landscapes. Expect to see palm trees and coastal views.

Infrastructure

Architecture is primarily traditional with thatched-roof houses, but there are also some modern buildings. Urban areas are small and not densely populated.

Culture

Cultural markers include traditional crafts, tapa cloth, and communal gatherings. Look for local markets and signs of Polynesian heritage.

Bonus

Look for distinctive flora such as hibiscus flowers and coconut palms, as well as small fishing boats and beach scenes.
