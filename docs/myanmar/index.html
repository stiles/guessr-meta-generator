Identity & symbols

Language

The official language is Burmese, which uses the Burmese script. Other languages include Shan, Karen, and Kachin, with various regional dialects.

Culture

Buddhist influences are prominent, with numerous pagodas and monasteries. Traditional attire, such as longyi (sarong), may be seen in rural areas.

Miscellaneous

Look for distinct three-wheeled taxis (trishaws or tuk-tuks) and local markets with vibrant displays of goods.

Capital

Nay Pyi Taw

Environment & landscape

Environment

Myanmar features diverse landscapes, including mountainous regions, lush forests, and rice paddies. The presence of pagodas and stupa-shaped structures is common.

Infrastructure

Buildings often reflect a mix of colonial, traditional Burmese, and modern architectural styles. Urban areas may have a combination of concrete structures and wooden houses.

Foliage

Common tree types include teak and rubber trees, along with various tropical hardwoods; grasses can include tall savanna grasses in open areas.

Topography

Myanmar features a diverse topography, including the rugged Shan Plateau, the Irrawaddy River valley, and coastal plains, with significant mountain ranges such as the Himalayas in the north.
