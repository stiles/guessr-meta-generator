Nigeria

Language

English is the official language; various local languages such as Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo are widely spoken.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a green stripe at the top showing 'Nigeria'. The plates often include a state code.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are typically in English, with blue, green, and yellow backgrounds; many signs include pictograms.

Environment

Diverse environments ranging from savannah and tropical rainforests to urban areas; notable features include red earth and palm trees.

Infrastructure

Mix of modern buildings and colonial architecture; urban centers have high-rise buildings, while rural areas feature mud houses and thatched roofs.

Culture

Vibrant street life, with markets and street vendors; distinctive clothing styles such as traditional fabrics (e.g., Ankara) and local crafts.

Bonus

Look for posters or billboards in English, as well as the presence of local street food vendors.
