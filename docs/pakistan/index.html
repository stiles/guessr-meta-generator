Pakistan

Language

Urdu is the national language, written in a modified Perso-Arabic script. English is also widely understood and used in official contexts.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a green strip on the left side. They typically have a provincial code and a numerical sequence.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are often bilingual (Urdu and English) and may include vibrant colors. They typically feature both Latin and Arabic scripts.

Environment

Diverse geographical features including mountains (e.g., the Himalayas), deserts (e.g., Thar), and river valleys (e.g., Indus River Valley).

Infrastructure

Architectural styles vary from modern buildings in urban areas to traditional mud-brick structures in rural areas. Notable features include domes and intricate tile work.

Culture

Cultural markers include the presence of mosques, traditional bazaars, and street food stalls. Clothing styles often reflect local customs.

Bonus

Street names in cities may be in Urdu or English, and the presence of rickshaws is common. Look for signs of local festivals or events.
