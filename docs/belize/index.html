Belize

Language

English is the official language; Spanish and Kriol are also widely spoken. The alphabet is the same as in English with no special characters.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a blue border, featuring the word 'Belize' at the top and a unique alphanumeric code.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are in English and often feature a mix of standard and unique designs, including some with colorful graphics and local themes.

Environment

The landscape includes coastal areas, rainforests, and barrier reefs, with tropical vegetation and diverse wildlife.

Infrastructure

Architecture is a mix of colonial styles, including wooden houses, and modern concrete buildings, often with vibrant colors.

Culture

Look for cultural markers like Garifuna music, Mayan ruins, and local markets that reflect the diverse heritage.

Bonus

Distinctive features include the presence of palm trees, Caribbean-style architecture, and the use of English in most public signage.
