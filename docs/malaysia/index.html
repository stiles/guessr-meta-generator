Malaysia

Language

Malay (Bahasa Malaysia) is the official language, using the Latin alphabet with diacritics for certain sounds.

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a black font, usually with a format of one letter followed by a series of numbers, and they often include a state code.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are typically in Malay and often include bilingual signs in English; signs are usually rectangular with blue, green, or yellow backgrounds.

Environment

Malaysia features tropical rainforests, mountains, and coastal areas, with distinct flora and fauna, such as palm trees and diverse wildlife.

Infrastructure

Modern urban architecture mixed with traditional Malay styles; buildings often include intricate designs and motifs, with a blend of colonial and contemporary influences.

Culture

Cultural markers include the presence of mosques, traditional markets, and street food stalls; festivals such as Hari Raya and Chinese New Year are widely celebrated.

Bonus

Look for unique street art, colorful murals, and signage in both Malay and English; tropical vegetation and distinct Malay houses (kampung) can also be indicators.
