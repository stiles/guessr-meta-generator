Identity & symbols

Language

English is the official language, with local languages such as Luganda, Runyankore, and others. The alphabet is Latin, with no special characters.

Culture

Distinctive markers include colorful markets, traditional attire, matoke (plantain) and local crafts. Look for cultural festivals and community gatherings.

Miscellaneous

Watch for roadside fruit vendors, the presence of boda bodas (motorcycle taxis), and wildlife signs indicating nearby national parks.

Capital

Kampala

Environment & landscape

Environment

The landscape features rolling hills, grasslands, and the Great Lakes region. Vegetation includes tropical forests and savannahs.

Infrastructure

Common architectural styles include brick houses with tin roofs and modern buildings in urban areas. Rural areas often have mud huts.

Coverage

True: Uganda has official Google Street View coverage in select urban areas.

Foliage

Common tree types include eucalyptus, acacia, and mango trees, along with grasses typical of savannah regions.

Topography

Uganda is characterized by a mix of mountainous regions, particularly around the Rwenzori Mountains, hilly areas, and flat plains, especially in the central region.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a yellow stripe on the left. They often begin with 'U' for Uganda.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the left side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are generally in English, often using a mix of standard international symbols and local styles. Warning signs are typically triangular.

Stop

Stop signs read 'STOP' in Uganda.

Bollards

Bollards in Uganda are typically simple and may be made of concrete or metal, often without reflective materials.

Poles

Utility poles in Uganda are generally wooden and may have multiple wires attached for electricity and telecommunications.
