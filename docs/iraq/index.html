Identity & symbols

Language

Arabic is the official language, written in a script that is right-to-left. Kurdish is also an official language in some regions, using both Latin and Arabic scripts.

Culture

Look for traditional markets (souks), local food stalls, and cultural symbols such as the Iraqi flag or references to Mesopotamian history.

Miscellaneous

Presence of palm trees, unique patterns in urban layouts, and traditional clothing can also be indicators.

Capital

Baghdad

Environment & landscape

Environment

Iraq features a mix of desert landscapes, river valleys (especially around the Tigris and Euphrates), and mountainous areas in the north.

Infrastructure

Architecture ranges from modern urban buildings to traditional mud-brick structures, with notable historical sites and mosques featuring domes and minarets.

Coverage

True: Iraq has official Google Street View coverage in some urban areas.

Foliage

Common tree types include date palms and olive trees, while grasses vary but are often adapted to arid conditions.

Topography

Iraq features a mix of flat plains, rolling hills, and mountainous regions, particularly in the north near the Zagros Mountains.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are white with a colored band at the top, usually blue or red, displaying the governorate name and vehicle type.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right-hand side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in Arabic, with some bilingual signs in Kurdish in northern regions; signs often feature a mix of Latin and Arabic numerals.

Stop

Stop signs typically read 'STOP' in English, as well as in Arabic (توقف).

Bollards

Bollards are generally simple and functional, often made of concrete or metal, and may not have unique designs.

Poles

Utility poles in Iraq are usually wooden or concrete, often carrying multiple lines of electricity and telecommunications, and may show signs of wear due to weather conditions.
