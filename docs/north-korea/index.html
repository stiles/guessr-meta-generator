Identity & symbols

Language

Korean (Hangul), which uses a unique script with characters that represent syllables.

Culture

Statues of Kim Il-sung and Kim Jong-il are common, as well as propaganda murals and posters. Public spaces are often quite organized and may lack the vibrancy seen in other countries.

Miscellaneous

The presence of military installations, lack of consumer goods, and unique state-run public transportation systems can be indicators. Limited internet and access to information can also be discerned.

Capital

Environment & landscape

Environment

The landscape features mountainous terrain, forests, and rivers, with agricultural fields in some areas. Urban areas may show stark contrasts due to limited development.

Infrastructure

Architecture is characterized by grandiose government buildings, monuments, and utilitarian structures. Notable styles include socialist realism and distinctive Pyongyang architecture.

Foliage

Common tree types include Korean pine and various deciduous trees, with grasses often found in rural areas.

Topography

North Korea is predominantly mountainous, with significant ranges such as the Taebaek Mountains, alongside some flat plains and coastal areas.

Roads & transportation

Cars

Vehicle registration plates are usually white with black letters, showing a combination of numbers and the province code. Plates often feature a simple design with minimal graphics.

Driving

Vehicles drive on the right side of the road.

Signs

Road signs are primarily in Hangul, with a simple and utilitarian design. Few signs are present, and they often lack the colorful graphics seen in other countries.

Stop

Stop signs in North Korea typically read '정지' (jeongji) which means 'stop' in Korean.

Bollards

Bollards in North Korea are often simple concrete posts with minimal decoration, typically painted in bright colors for visibility.

Poles

Utility poles in North Korea are generally wooden and may have minimal markings; some are used for both electricity and telephone lines.
